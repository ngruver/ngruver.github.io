Nate Gruver

I am a second year PhD student in computer science at NYU Courant, where I work on machine learning. I am advised by Andrew Gordon Wilson and Kyunghyun Cho.

In industry I have worked on behavior modeling at Waymo, language models at Apple, and reinforcement learning on the Kernel team at Google Cloud.

I received a BS and MS in computer science from Stanford University. While at Stanford, I worked on deep latent variable models, imitation learning and probabilistic planning with Stefano Ermon, Mykel Kochenderfer, and Chris Piech.

Research

I work primarily on deep learning. My ongoing work mostly involves

  1. Experimentally analyzing the role of inductive biases in deep learning [1]
  2. Machine learning for protein design [2]
  3. Improving dynamics models for planning/control, both of agents [3, 4] and mechanical systems [5, 1]
